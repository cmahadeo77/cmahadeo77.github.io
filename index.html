Partnership Development

How I build upstream

Most BD work fails at the structural level — not because relationships aren't built, but because the partnership economics aren't designed to survive contact with the other party's P&L. My approach starts with how each party makes money, then works backward to what the partnership needs to look like for it to last. I have done this at Visa, at Mastercard, and now operationally at Arikina — across payment networks, fintech platforms, ingredient suppliers, and distribution partners.

Upstream
Network & Platform Partners

Card networks, core processors, BaaS platforms, stablecoin rails. Relationships at this layer define the economics everything downstream inherits. I have operated here — not just advised on it.

Midstream
Fintech & Issuer Partnerships

Fintechs building on rails, embedded finance players, issuers expanding into new verticals. The deal structures here require fluency on both the commercial and technical sides simultaneously.

Downstream
Distribution & Commercial Partners

Merchants, distributors, ingredient suppliers, and brand channels. Operational partnership experience — real contracts, real negotiations, real consequences. Grounded in running Arikina day-to-day.

Partnership development framework
01
Ecosystem Mapping
Weeks 1–2
Identify who controls leverage in the target ecosystem. Map the P&L flow — who captures interchange, who owns the relationship, who has the switching cost. Surfaces the right partners before outreach begins.
02
Value Architecture
Weeks 2–3
Design the commercial structure from both sides of the table. What does the partner gain economically? What do they give up? Where are the natural misalignments, and how do you build around them before the term sheet?
03
Warm Entry
Weeks 3–5
Network-native introductions where possible. Cold outreach where not — but framed around their business context, not a pitch deck. Entry through working-level relationships before escalating to decision-makers.
04
Commercial Structuring
Weeks 5–8
Revenue share, volume incentives, exclusivity windows, MFN clauses, co-marketing commitments. Structuring that closes and survives — not just terms that get signed and then ignored at first renewal.
05
Activation & Governance
Ongoing
Launch playbook, joint GTM motion, QBR cadence, and escalation paths. Partnerships that don't activate die quietly — governance keeps them alive and compounding.
